The Three I's

Integrity

To be constantly aware of what is right and wrong and to consistently think and act according to what is right.

Industry

The habit of sincere, steady, and continued devotion to any useful or productive work, whether physical or mental.

Intelligence

The ability to exercise the higher mental functions, to grasp the significant factors of a complex problem or situation, and the capacity to meet situations--especially if new or unforeseen--by a rapid and effective adjustment of behavior.
